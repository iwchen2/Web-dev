What are Cats?

The domestic cat is a small, typically furry, carnivorous mammal. They are often called house cats when kept as indoor pets or simply cats. Cats are often valued by humans for companionship and for their ability to hunt vermin. There are more than 70 cat breeds, though different associations proclaim different numbers according to their standards. Cats have a very unique anatomy, with a strong flexible body, quick reflexes, sharp retractable claws, and teeth adapted to killing small prey. They can see in darkness. Despite being solitary hunters, cats are a social species and have a special way of communicating using vocalizations, pheromones, and body-language. As of a 2007 study, cats are the second most popular pet in the US by number of pets owned, behind freshwater fish. In a 2010 study they were ranked the third most popular pet in the UK, after fish and dogs, with around 8 million being owned. First domestication of cats reaches as early as ancient Egypt, but the true origin of cats dates before that in the Neolithic period about 9,500 years ago. Behavior

Feeding

For domestic cats, their food selection is based on temperature, smell and texture; They dislike cold foods and prefer moist foods rich in amino acids. Put in simple words, cats are meat-eaters. Some common food types that cats cosume are beef, chicken, turkey, tuna, etc. The protein helps give them a strong heart, good vision, and healthy reproductive system. Stick to the commercial cat food bought at stores, and look out for the labels that say "balanced diet" to ensure your cat gets all of its nutrients needed. Although the diet of your cat should be predominatly(at least 70%) cat food, a snack from your plate can be given to them in small amounts. Cats will often eat their main meals at dusk and dawn, when they would normally be hunting and catching prey in the wild. Similar to humans, the portion size of your cats food should be relative to her age, size, and activities throughtout the day. An average cat should consume about 200 calories.

Caring

Some of the essential needs for a cat are food and water, shelter, litter box, and your unconditional love. If provided, you are on your way to a healthy relationship with your feline friend. Remember to take your cat regularly to your local veterinarian to ensure vaccinations are up to date. Cats will instinctively use the litter box; just remember to scoop it daily and rinse with hot water and dish soap periodically. Cats love being groomed, and this also keeps their coats and skin healthy, prevents matting, and reduces shedding. Their claws also have to be clipped to keep them from growing into their toenails.
